Australian Standard AS3745-2010 sets the benchmark for emergency planning in facilities across the country. Whether you manage a commercial office tower, a university campus, or a mining operation, compliance is not optional -- it is a legal obligation under WHS legislation.

## What AS3745 Requires

At its core, AS3745 mandates that every facility must have:

- **An Emergency Planning Committee (EPC)** responsible for developing, implementing, and maintaining the emergency plan
- **A written Emergency Response Plan** that covers evacuation procedures, shelter-in-place protocols, and communication chains
- **Trained wardens** at every level -- Chief Wardens, Floor/Area Wardens, and First Aid Officers
- **Regular drills and exercises** to test the plan and train occupants
- **Post-incident reviews** to capture lessons and improve the plan

## Where Most Facilities Fall Short

In practice, many facilities struggle with three areas:

### 1. Warden Training Gaps

AS3745 requires wardens to be trained before they assume their role, and refresher training must occur regularly. Paper-based tracking makes it easy for certifications to lapse without anyone noticing. When an auditor checks your records, "we think they were trained last year" is not an acceptable answer.

### 2. Drill Documentation

Running a drill is only half the requirement. You must record attendance, timing, outcomes, and corrective actions. Clipboard-based headcounts are slow, error-prone, and difficult to audit. A facility with 500 occupants using manual roll calls can take 15-20 minutes to confirm all-clear -- time that matters in a real emergency.

### 3. Continuous Improvement

AS3745 expects a closed-loop process: plan, execute, review, improve. Most facilities treat emergency management as an annual checkbox rather than an ongoing programme. Hazard reports sit in inboxes. Drill findings never make it into plan revisions.

## Building a Compliant Framework

A robust AS3745 compliance framework includes:

1. **Digital hazard capture** -- report hazards from any device, with photos, location data, and automatic routing to the right person
2. **Warden management** -- track training status, certifications, and role assignments in real time
3. **Drill planning and execution** -- schedule drills, run real-time headcounts, and generate audit-ready reports automatically
4. **Emergency dashboards** -- during a live event, see who is accounted for, who is missing, and what actions are underway
5. **Auditable records** -- every action, decision, and outcome is logged with timestamps for regulators

## The Cost of Non-Compliance

Beyond the risk of regulatory fines, non-compliance carries real consequences. A facility that cannot account for its occupants during an emergency puts lives at risk. Insurers are increasingly asking for evidence of AS3745 compliance, and WorkSafe inspectors are looking beyond the plan document to assess whether the plan is actually exercised and improved.
